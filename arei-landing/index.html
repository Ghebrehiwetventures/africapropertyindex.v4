The gap

The market exists.
The data layer does not.

Fragmentation

Listings sit across brokers, Facebook groups, portals, and classifieds — each with its own format and language.

No comparability

Euros in Praia, shillings in Nairobi, cedis in Accra. No common structure for price, type, or location.

Nothing to build on

No API, no feed, no structured dataset. Every analysis starts from scratch.

The engine

We don't sell properties.
We structure the data behind them.

Our engine crawls brokers, portals, and classifieds across markets — then normalizes every listing into a clean, structured format. Price, location, type, and area are extracted regardless of language. Each record is deduplicated and scored before it enters the index.

Why now

This wasn't possible three years ago.
The window won't stay open.

Three structural shifts converged at the same moment. No incumbent has moved yet.

AI removed the cost barrier

Normalization across Portuguese, French, Arabic, and Swahili no longer requires a parser per language. LLMs solve this at scale.

The position is still open

There is no Zillow for Africa. No CoStar. No pan-African property data layer of any kind. The index position is unclaimed.

Each market compounds the moat

Better data attracts developers and lenders. Developer demand creates monetization. Each new market makes the next one cheaper to launch.
