Market Opportunity

Africa's property market is enormous — and nearly unmapped.

African real estate represents one of the largest untapped data opportunities on earth. Rapid urbanization, a growing middle class, and rising institutional capital flows are converging — but the infrastructure to make sense of it barely exists at scale. Local portals exist in some markets. What does not exist is a normalized, cross-source, pan-African index layer. That is the gap AREI fills.

Why Now

Four tailwinds that make this the right moment.

The infrastructure to capture this opportunity — scraping, normalization, cloud data pipelines, LLM-assisted parsing — is mature enough today to do at low cost what would have required a large team five years ago.

Fastest urbanising continent

Africa's urban population is growing at 4%+ per year. More city-dwellers means more property transactions, more search intent, more data demand.

Mobile-first internet growth

Smartphone penetration across Africa is rising 15% YoY. Property search is moving online — but the products to serve it don't exist yet.

Institutional capital entering

Global real estate funds are allocating to Africa for the first time. They need market intelligence. Right now there is almost none.

AI makes parsing cheap

LLM-assisted data extraction means we can normalize fragmented listing data across languages, formats, and currencies at near-zero marginal cost.

No incumbent to displace

CoStar took 30 years to become a $30B company in a market that already had data. Africa has no incumbent. The index layer is wide open.

Data network effects compound

The first mover who builds clean, normalized, multi-market data creates a moat that gets stronger with every source added and every market entered.

How It Works

Not a marketplace.
A data layer.

AREI sits beneath consumer products. We discover sources, ingest listings, normalize structure, and enforce trust rules before anything reaches a public surface. Products like KazaVerde sit on top. So can B2B APIs, market reports, and third-party tools.

01 — Discover

Source coverage

We crawl brokers, portals, developer sites, and classifieds. More sources means better coverage — but only if the output stays usable.

→
02 — Normalise

Structured truth

Price, location, area, type, and source are extracted and standardised across formats, languages, and currencies. Comparison becomes possible.

→
03 — Trust

Quality enforcement

Deduplication, completeness scoring, and source verification. We publish only what passes — no dumping raw volume into public surfaces.

→
The Moat

A data flywheel that compounds over time.

The more markets we index, the more valuable the platform becomes to users. More users surface better data signals. Better signals attract more sources. This is the same defensibility loop that made CoStar and Zillow nearly impossible to displace.

01

More sources → richer coverage

Each new broker, portal, or developer site we ingest increases coverage. The index becomes more complete and more trustworthy.

02

Better data → better products

More complete data means better search, better price signals, better market context. Consumer and B2B surfaces improve automatically.

03

Better products → more users

Superior products attract more search traffic, more agents, and more institutional clients who rely on the index for market intelligence.

04

More users → more sources

User engagement surfaces data gaps. New sources approach us. The index compounds — harder to replicate with each new market and source added.
